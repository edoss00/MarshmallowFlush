Virus Spread Simulation

Settings

After this many people have been infected, social distancing measures begin to take effect.
In any given moment, a person has this chance of receiving the disease from someone in their vicinity.

This simulation displays a simple visualization of what the spread of a disease through a community can look like. Each circle represents a human. Black dots represent uninfected people, yellow dots represent infected people who don't yet show symptoms, and red dots represent people showing symptoms. It is worth noting thatwhen dots disappear, it does not necessarily mean that they have died. It means that they've been "removed" from the population, which means they may have died or may be in a hospital and could potentially recover.
